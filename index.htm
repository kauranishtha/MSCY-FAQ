<h1><a name="_Toc534342433">Before Applying to the MSCY Program</a></h1>

<p><b>1. Is the Master's in Cybersecurity program accredited?</b></p>

<p>Northeastern University has been designated by the National Security Agency and the U.S.</p>

<p>Department of Homeland Security as a Center of Academic Excellence in Information</p>

<p>Assurance/Cyber Defense, IA Research, and Cyber Operations.</p>

<p><b>2. What are the number of credits required for graduation?</b></p>

<p>The Masters in Cyber Security degree program requires 32 credits for graduation. There are 2 </p>

<p>tracks namely technical and contextual which should be completed in order to graduate. A detailed description of the courses aligned which each of the track and electives can be found in the following link: <href="#programrequirementstext">http://catalog.northeastern.edu/graduate/computer-information-science/information-assurance/cybersecurity-ms/#programrequirementstext</a><u ><br />
</p>

<p><b>3. How many courses will I need to take to graduate?</b></p>

<p>All the students enrolled for Masters in Cyber Security need to take IA5010 Foundation of </p>

<p>Information Assurance (4 credit), minimum 8 credits from Technical course, minimum 8 credits </p>

<p>from Contextual Course and Capstone Couse for graduation. However, Capstone requirement </p>

<p>can be waived by students taking either a. Research Project on National Information Security </p>

<p>Course b. an IA Research Project course and a Master of Science in Cyber Security thesis course </p>

<p>for a total of two semester.</p>

<p><b>10. Is it possible to do join degrees while pursuing MSCY?</b></p>

<p>Students may complete a Master of Science in Cybersecurity in addition to earning a Graduate Certificate in Engineering Leadership. The integrated 40-semester-hour master’s degree and certificate require 24 hours of information assurance course work. <p><b>29. What are Co-ops? How to get one? And what are the benefits of doing a Co-op? </b></p>

<p>Co-op stands for Cooperative Education Experience which is essentially an opportunity to work for 6-8 months full-time (i.e., using one full semester for doing a co-op instead of talking courses). For international students, this work experience will be counted under their CPT time. Co-ops are different from internship such that an internship is a 2-4 months long work experience whereas a Co-op can be 6-8 months long. During a co-op, a student generally does not enroll for any full-time courses but there is an option to opt for a course in the second half of the co-op. </p>

<p>The process of finding a co-op works in a similar way as finding any other job. Northeastern University has its own database called NU Careers where job posting for Co-ops are listed and students can apply. Students are also allowed to find a co-op from other sources as well such as LinkedIn and other job boards. The professors at University and alumni are also quite resourceful while looking for co-ops. CCIS provides a “Co-op Experience” class in first semester which helps students to prepare for their upcoming co-op search process and how to approach it. </p>

<p>Co-ops are extremely beneficial as most of the times, students are offered full-time jobs after the completion of their co-op. It helps to understand the practical application of concepts taught in the courses in MSCY. For international students, co-op work experience helps them to get acquainted with American work culture. Generally, it is seen that once the students complete their co-op, they can streamline their interests more easily and then carve their career path accordingly. </p>

<p><b>35. What is a Capstone Project?</b></p>

<p>In a capstone project, students will work on a cybersecurity project provided by a company. Students can reach out companies for projects themselves or industry partners of the college would provide projects for students to choose from. A group a students work together on a project (online or on campus) and provide a research report or a presentation (or both) at the end of the course. During the course, the instructor would follow up with teams' progress and answer questions.</p>

<p>The program accepts students from wide variety of academic and professional backgrounds. There are many courses where knowledge about Python will come in handy. Although, the programming skill will be helpful, but the program does not require deep programing knowledge. </p>

<p>The course structure, instructors and Co-op program really make the program stand out. The program provides a wide range of courses for students to choose from. The courses cover different fields in Cybersecurity: computer system security, network security, software security, risk analysis, forensics, cryptographic and etc. Students can choose classes based on their interest and career plans.</p>

<p>Instructors in the program are also practitioners in the industry. Many of them has years of work experience in Cybersecurity.
